### Chart 3: What are the critical questions in our State?"

In the Prince Edward Island School System today, a place where learning is highly valued, all individuals have the opportunity to develop their full potential. The Department of Education and Early Childhood Development, the leader in opportunities for lifelong learning, emphasizes:

- Are resources ensuring equitable access for all students utilized appropriately?
- How are the literacy levels of the population with Special Educational Needs being addressed?
- How is the education system utilizing technology to support students with Special Educational Needs?

This statement, focusing on lifelong education for all individuals, aligns with the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms, which also commits the province of Prince Edward Island to providing equal access to education for all.

The 1980s marked a turning point for the province, as initiatives for individuals with Special Educational Needs were introduced, leading to the legal anchoring of their rights in the Charter of Rights and Freedoms. This phase meant that children with Special Educational Needs could attend mainstream schools tailored to their individual requirements.

The Prince Edward Island School Act outlines the educational organization of the province, emphasizing inclusive education without the need for separate schooling for children and youth with Special Educational Needs.

The Department of Education and Early Childhood Development is committed to providing appropriate educational offerings for students with special needs based on the philosophy of inclusion.

Furthermore, the establishment of inclusive structures in Prince Edward Island is ongoing, ensuring that support measures and structures for students with Special Educational Needs are described and relevant terms are specified.

Prince Edward Island, like other Canadian provinces and territories, is defined as a province with a cosmopolitan societal structure. While the English language predominates, the French-speaking population is also represented, with provisions made for French-language education through the School Board and comprehensive French-Immersion Programs.
